RockBot App/Interaction Redesign (UI/IX)

Workflow:

To kick off the redesign, I first reviewed the existing app to identify where users might lose interest and how the overall vibe could better reflect musics energetic and social nature. I then mapped out a user persona to capture the type of people most likely to use this app—students, young adults, and music lovers looking for a fun, communal experience. From there, I sketched wireframes to rethink the navigation and interaction flow, while also experimenting with color palettes, typography, and micro-animations that could bring the interface to life. Once I finalized the design direction, I created high-fidelity mockups and added engaging motion elements to make the app feel vibrant, exciting, and fun to use.

YouCue is a music cueing app that lets users pick and queue songs at different locations. While the concept is fun, the existing design felt dated and lacked energy. The visuals were flat, and there wasnt much happening in terms of interaction or motion. This made the app feel less engaging and didnt match the lively, social vibe that music naturally brings. I set out to redesign the app with a fresh look, bringing in a more fun and hip atmosphere through playful animations, smoother interactions, and a more dynamic layout!

Our initial wireframes focused on the flow of information. We wanted the user to feel guided by the typography, using size and weight to establish a clear hierarchy of importance from the very first click.

The technical implementation relied heavily on CSS Grid and Flexbox to ensure that the layout remained symmetrical across ultra-wide monitors and mobile devices alike, maintaining the signature zigzag rhythm.

Data visualization was a key component of this project. We developed custom SVG charts that inherit the brand's primary and accent colors, ensuring the analytics felt like an integrated part of the design.

The final testing phase involved rigorous accessibility audits. By using high-contrast colors like our signature blue against the navy background, we ensured all content passed WCAG 2.1 AA standards for readability.
